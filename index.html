Children's Healthy Development Study

This study tracks your child's growth and development over the next three years to understand which environmental and social factors shape their developmental outcomes.

📖 Project Background & Objectives

Housing provides an important physical and social environment for children's development, social-emotional functioning, and academic performance. Young children grow exponentially and spend considerable time at home, making the role of housing crucial. Given the socioeconomic disparities and pressing housing difficulties in Hong Kong, more cross-sectoral efforts are urged for the well-being of low-income families.

Drawing on the bioecological model of human development, we will track children aged two to six across three waves to gather empirical evidence to inform housing interventions and contribute to a child-friendly city.
